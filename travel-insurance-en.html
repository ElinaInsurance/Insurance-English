02 / Two Coverage Directions

Leaving Canada & Arriving in Canada:
Both Directions Need Coverage

Travel insurance protects two entirely different groups — not just Canadians travelling abroad, but also visiting family members who face the same risk of sky-high medical bills:

✈️
Canadians Going Abroad
Travel to the US, Europe, Asia, or anywhere outside Canada — provincial health is ineffective abroad. Purchase before departure.
🏠
Visitors Coming to Canada
Parents, relatives visiting on a visitor visa or Super Visa — the Canadian government does not cover visitors' medical bills and strongly recommends insurance.
🚨
Visiting Parents Face the Highest Risk
If your parents visit from China, India, or elsewhere and suffer a heart attack, stroke, or fall, a single hospitalization can cost over $100,000 CAD. The Canadian government strongly recommends all visitors purchase health insurance before arrival.
📋
Super Visa Legally Requires Insurance
The Super Visa (for parents/grandparents) legally requires at least $100,000 CAD of medical coverage from a Canadian insurance company, valid for at least one year.
🌏
Inter-Provincial Travel Also Has Gaps
Travelling within Canada across provinces? There are gaps in inter-provincial health coverage. Manulife offers a dedicated Travel Canada Emergency Medical plan for exactly this scenario.
🔑 The Simple Truth
Canada's public health system covers Canadian residents, in Canada. The moment someone crosses a border — or isn't a covered resident — only travel insurance stands between them and financial ruin.
03 / What's Covered

Travel Insurance:
Six Types of Protection

Travel insurance is a comprehensive package protecting against unexpected medical emergencies and travel disruptions:

🏥
Emergency Medical (Core Coverage)
Hospital, surgery, ER, ambulance, X-rays, prescriptions for unexpected illness or injury — up to $5M CAD with top-tier plans
✈️
Trip Cancellation & Interruption
Non-refundable flights, hotels, tours if you must cancel or cut the trip short due to illness, death, or covered emergency
🧳
Baggage Loss & Delay
Reimbursement for lost, stolen, or delayed luggage and essential replacement items
🚁
Medical Evacuation & Repatriation
When needed, arrange and fund medical air transport back to Canada with medical escort
⚡
Accidental Death & Dismemberment
Lump-sum payment to beneficiaries in the event of accidental death or permanent disability during travel
🦷
Emergency Dental
Covers emergency dental treatment caused by an accidental blow to natural teeth while travelling
💡 All-Inclusive vs Emergency Medical Only
All-Inclusive: Medical + trip cancellation + baggage + accident — best for long trips and expensive bookings. Emergency Medical Only: Pure health coverage at lower cost — best for visiting relatives or budget-conscious short trips.

08 / In an Emergency

Medical Emergency Abroad:
What to Do First

If a medical emergency occurs while travelling, the sequence of your actions matters enormously — the wrong order can lead to claim denial:

1
Call the 24/7 Emergency Assistance Line FIRST
Before going to a hospital, call your insurer's emergency hotline. Save this number in your phone before departure. All three providers — TuGo, Allianz, Manulife — offer global 24/7 emergency lines.
2
Get Pre-Authorized and Directed to a Network Hospital
The insurer will direct you to a preferred hospital and arrange direct billing, so you don't need to pay out-of-pocket and file for reimbursement later. Unauthorized treatment may not be fully covered.
3
Keep All Receipts and Medical Records
Save every prescription, receipt, diagnosis, and discharge summary. Complete medical documentation is essential for any claim you may need to file for self-paid expenses.
4
Submit Claim After Return
Submit your claim online (TuGo, Manulife) or via the Allianz app. Most claims are processed within 30 days of receiving complete documentation.
⚠️ Most Common Reasons Claims Are Denied
• Failure to disclose pre-existing conditions at time of purchase (most common)
• Not contacting the insurer before seeking treatment
• Symptoms existed before the policy was purchased
• Travelling to a destination under a Government of Canada travel advisory
• Policy expired or not renewed before travel dates
09 / Buying Guide

How to Buy Correctly:
7 Non-Negotiable Rules

💰
Get At Least $1M CAD in Coverage
US medical costs are catastrophic. $100K or $200K isn't nearly enough for a serious event. Visitors applying for a Super Visa must carry minimum $1M CAD by law.
📅
Buy Before Departure — Always
All insurers require purchase before leaving home. You cannot buy new coverage once you've departed, and pre-existing symptoms that emerge mid-trip won't be covered retrospectively.
🩺
Disclose All Pre-Existing Conditions Honestly
Don't hide hypertension, diabetes, or heart conditions. Honest disclosure ensures your claim is paid. Concealment gives the insurer grounds to deny any claim — even for unrelated incidents.
🗺️
Confirm Your Destination Is Covered
Policies may exclude destinations under a Government of Canada travel advisory (certain Middle East regions, conflict zones). Verify before purchasing.
🔁
Travelling More Than Twice a Year? Get Annual
Annual multi-trip plans from TuGo, Allianz, and Manulife are typically 30–50% cheaper than purchasing separate single-trip coverage each time.
📞
Save the Emergency Number Before You Leave
Store the 24/7 emergency assistance hotline in your phone. Screenshot your policy. Tell family members your policy number in case of emergency.
🤝
Buy Through a Licensed Advisor
An insurance advisor understands policy differences and can recommend the right plan based on your specific health profile and travel destination — not just the cheapest option.
