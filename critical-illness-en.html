03 / How It Works

Critical Illness Insurance:
Simple. Powerful. Tax-Free.

Critical illness insurance is a diagnosis-triggered living benefit. If you're diagnosed with a covered condition, you receive a one-time, tax-free lump-sum payment — no receipts required, no restrictions on how you spend it.

1
Choose Your Coverage Amount & Pay Premiums
Select a coverage amount based on your income, debts, and family needs (typically $50,000–$500,000). Pay regular premiums to keep the policy active.
2
Receive a Covered Critical Illness Diagnosis
Your doctor diagnoses you with a condition listed in your policy (e.g., cancer, heart attack, stroke). Most policies require a survival period (typically 30 days) before you can file a claim.
3
File Your Claim
Submit your diagnosis confirmation and medical documentation to the insurer for review and approval.
4
Receive Your Tax-Free Lump-Sum Payment
Once approved, you receive the full benefit in one payment. Completely tax-free, spend it however you choose — pay off debt, cover medications, hire help, or anything else you need.
🔑 The Key: You Decide How to Use It
Unlike medical reimbursement plans, CI insurance requires no receipts and no justification. Pay your mortgage, replace lost income, seek treatment abroad, or hire a caregiver — completely your choice.

06 / CI vs Disability Insurance

Critical Illness vs Disability:
Do You Need Both?

Many people confuse CI insurance with disability insurance. They protect differently — they complement, not replace, each other:

Feature Critical Illness Disability Insurance
Payment typeOne-time tax-free lump sumMonthly income replacement
TriggerDiagnosed with covered conditionUnable to work due to illness/injury
Waiting periodNone in some casesUsually 30–90 day wait
Coverage durationLifelong coverageTypically ends at age 65
Best forLarge lump-sum needs: drugs, mortgage payoff, major expensesOngoing monthly living expenses
✅ Best Strategy: Use Both Together
CI insurance provides a large one-time cash injection for immediate high-cost needs. Disability insurance ensures steady monthly income to maintain your lifestyle over the long term. Together, they form a complete financial safety net against serious illness.
🎯
Especially Critical for the Self-Employed
Many employees have group CI insurance through their employer — but freelancers, contractors, and business owners have no group coverage. For the self-employed, individual CI insurance is an essential personal safety net with no employer backup.

📋
Why Claims Get Denied — And How to Avoid It
Munich Re's 2024 survey found 71% of denied CI claims were rejected because the condition didn't meet the policy definition or wasn't listed. To protect yourself:

• Read condition definitions carefully (e.g., early-stage vs. late-stage cancer)
• Confirm exactly which conditions are covered
• Disclose all pre-existing conditions honestly
• Work with a licensed advisor to find the right policy
⚡ Most Important Recommendation
CI insurance policies are complex. Condition definitions and coverage vary significantly between insurers. Always work with a licensed advisor to compare multiple insurers' terms and find the policy that best matches your specific health situation and financial needs.
