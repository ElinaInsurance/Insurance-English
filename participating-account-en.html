The Basics

What Is a Participating Account?

The investment engine inside participating life insurance

🏦
Legally Separate & Protected
Under the Insurance Companies Act, Canada Life maintains the participating account completely separate from other company assets — dedicated exclusively to participating policyholders.
📊
How Are Dividends Generated?
When actual experience in investment returns, mortality, expenses, and taxes outperforms pricing assumptions, the surplus is distributed as policyholder dividends.
🔄
Bigger & Stronger Since 2020
On Jan. 1, 2020, Canada Life merged three separate participating accounts into one larger combined account — greater risk diversification, lower management costs, more stable dividends.
📌 Board Oversight
The Board of Directors approves investment policies annually. The Appointed Actuary reports each year on the fairness and equitableness of expense and income allocations.

The participating account uses a multi-year smoothing method to spread investment gains and losses over time — shielding your dividends from short-term market swings. This is a structural advantage GICs simply cannot replicate.
Mechanism

How Does the Account Work?

Diversified assets + professional management + smoothing

1
Asset Allocation
Assets are split into near-term (next 10 years) — primarily fixed income — and long-term investments for growth
2
Diversified Long-Term Pool
Long-term assets include fixed income, common stocks, private equity, and real estate — broad diversification, lower total risk
3
Return Smoothing
Multi-year smoothing averages investment returns across periods — the dividend scale interest rate changes gradually, not abruptly
4
Annual Dividend Review
Each year the Appointed Actuary evaluates investment, mortality, expenses, and taxes to set a fair dividend scale for policyholders
5
Board-Level Governance
The Board approves investment policies annually and receives fairness reports — ensuring policyholder interests are protected at every level
