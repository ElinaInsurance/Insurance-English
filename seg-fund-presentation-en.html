01 / The Basics

What Is a
Segregated Fund?

🏛️
Core Definition
A Segregated Fund is a variable annuity contract issued exclusively by life insurance companies. Your premiums are invested in a professionally managed portfolio of stocks, bonds, and other assets — but the fund is kept strictly separate from the insurer's general assets, protected under provincial Insurance Acts.
⚖️
Legal Standing
Under the Canadian Income Tax Act, a Seg Fund is treated as a life insurance contract (Para 138.1) and regulated by provincial Insurance Acts — not securities law. This critical distinction unlocks a set of legal protections unavailable to any other investment vehicle.
🛡️
Core Guarantee Mechanism
The insurance company must guarantee the return of at least 75% of premiums paid (some contracts guarantee 100%) at the contract's maturity date or upon the death of the annuitant — a protection no Mutual Fund or ETF can offer.

Standard estate administration can take months or even years. Seg Fund beneficiaries typically receive funds within two weeks — money that can immediately cover funeral costs, taxes, and other urgent obligations.

06 / Tax Advantages

Tax Benefits
You Can't Ignore

LOSS
FLOW-THROUGH
Capital Losses Flow Directly to Investors
A Seg Fund's capital losses can be allocated to investors at year-end, allowing you to offset capital gains and reduce your tax bill. Mutual Fund losses stay trapped inside the fund and are never passed on to unitholders.
TAX
FLOW-THROUGH
Income Character Is Preserved
Seg Funds act as a "tax conduit" — dividends, interest, and capital gains retain their original tax character when passed to investors via a T3 slip. Capital gains benefit from the 50% inclusion rate advantage.
PURCHASE
TIMING
No "Phantom Tax" on Late-Year Purchases
Some Seg Funds prorate income allocation based on how long you held the units. With Mutual Funds, buying at year-end can trigger tax on a full year's income you never benefited from — a trap Seg Funds can avoid.
DEATH
BENEFIT
Death Benefits Received Tax-Efficiently
Insurance proceeds paid to beneficiaries flow outside the estate, ensuring the full amount reaches the beneficiary without being reduced by income tax on the estate itself.
RRSP /
TFSA
Fully Compatible with Registered Accounts
Seg Funds work seamlessly inside RRSP, RRIF, and TFSA accounts, stacking registered account tax deferral or tax-free growth on top of the insurance protections — dual-layer tax optimization.
⚠️ Key Tax Difference vs. Mutual Funds
Mutual Fund capital losses never reach investors — they remain inside the fund to offset future gains. Seg Fund loss flow-through is a meaningful tax buffer for non-registered investors during down markets.

08 / Multi-Layer Protection

Seg Fund's
Five-Layer Protection System

🔵
Layer 1: Maturity Principal Guarantee
At the contract's maturity date (typically after 10 years), the insurer guarantees the return of at least 75% of premiums paid (up to 100% in some products). Even if the fund's market value falls to zero, you receive the guaranteed amount.
🟡
Layer 2: Death Benefit Guarantee
Upon the policyholder's death, beneficiaries receive the greater of the current market value or the guaranteed amount (75–100% of premiums), protecting heirs from the misfortune of poor market timing at death.
🟢
Layer 3: Guarantee Reset Option
When the fund's net asset value rises, investors may voluntarily reset the guarantee base to the new, higher market value — locking in investment gains as the new guarantee floor so protections grow with your wealth.
🔴
Layer 4: Creditor Protection
When a preferred family member (spouse, child, parent) is named as beneficiary, Seg Fund assets may be exempt from creditor seizure in bankruptcy or civil lawsuits — an essential safeguard for business owners and self-employed individuals.
🟣
Layer 5: ASSURIS Regulatory Protection
Even if the issuing life insurance company becomes insolvent, ASSURIS — Canada's independent life insurance protection organization — guarantees policyholders will receive at least 85% of their policy benefits (minimum $60,000).
